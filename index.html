The problem

INC registration does not equal procedural competency

Research across Uttarakhand and North India hospital cohorts shows GNP-qualified nurses entering private hospital wards with near-zero independent procedural confidence in high-risk skills. The registration examination does not assess practical competency at the level required for unsupervised patient contact.

The program

A complete, deployable training architecture

13 weeks. 39 in-person sessions at 3 per week, 2 hours each. Blended delivery combining simulation, scenario-based practice, and structured self-directed learning. Your training department runs it. No additional curriculum development required.

Critical modules

M3 BLS/CPR — M4 Defibrillator/AED — M5 Neonatal resuscitation

100% pass on critical steps. Minimum 3 supervised attempts. Mandatory remediation before any patient contact on failure. Gate 1 hard stop at end of Week 4.

High skill modules

M6–M14: Airway, Medication, IV cannulation, Phlebotomy, ECG, Catheterisation, NG tube, Wound care, Perioperative

80% pass threshold, zero critical errors permitted. Minimum 2 supervised attempts. Three gate assessments across the program.

Foundations

M1 Infection control and patient ID — M2 Clinical communication and documentation

Prerequisites for all procedural modules. Bilingual Hindi/English SDL workbooks for North India cohorts. 3–4 week production lead time required before program start.

Final assessment

M15 Integrated OSCE

4–6 station circuit covering all critical skills, sampled high skills, and SBAR handover. Three-signature Competency Passport sign-off within 5 working days. No partial passes issued.

13-week timeline

Four assessment gates. No nurse proceeds without passing.

Weeks 1–2

Foundations and communication

M1: Infection control, hand hygiene, PPE, patient identification. M2: SBAR, clinical documentation, bilingual SDL workbook for North India cohorts.

Weeks 3–4

Critical skills block

M3 BLS/CPR, M4 Defibrillator/AED, M5 Neonatal resuscitation. Adult and neonatal manikin simulation. Minimum 3 supervised attempts per skill.

Gate 1

End of Week 4 — Critical skills

Hard stop. No ward contact in any critical skill area until passed. Mandatory remediation on failure regardless of cohort timeline.

Weeks 5–8

Foundation high skills

M6 Airway and O2 therapy, M7 Medication administration, M8 IV cannulation, M9 Phlebotomy, M10 ECG recording. Supervised bedside practice for M8 and M9 where ward access is confirmed.

Gate 2

End of Week 8 — Foundation high skills

Written and practical assessments for M6–M10. Targeted remediation on failed steps only.

Weeks 9–11

Procedural high skills

M11 Urinary catheterisation (male and female), M12 NG tube insertion and enteral feeding, M13 Wound dressing and pressure injury management.

Gate 3

End of Week 11 — Procedural high skills

Written and practical assessments for M11–M13. Full module repeat required on second failed reassessment.

Week 12

Perioperative nursing care

M14: Pre-op, intra-op, post-op/PACU. WHO Surgical Safety Checklist SDL workbook. Scenario-based simulation reflecting deploying hospital OT workflow.

Gate 4 / Week 13

Integrated OSCE and Competency Passport sign-off

M15: 4–6 station OSCE circuit. Three-signature Competency Passport issued within 5 working days of final session.

What the hospital gets

Outputs built for hospital operations and compliance

Competency Passport per nurse

Module-by-module pass record with written scores, practical scores, supervised attempt counts, and three assessor signatures. Issued at program completion, not provisionally.

INC CNE credit hours

40–50 INC Continuing Nursing Education hours per participant. Maps to the correct regulatory framework for INC-registered nurses. Not NSDC/HSSC, which is designed for pre-service vocational skilling.

NABH and JCI audit trail

Source-mapping appendix linking every module competency to MoHFW, INC, WHO, or AIIMS standard. Cohort completion summary with gate pass rates and remediation data.

No curriculum development required

Complete program architecture, competency checklists, SDL workbooks, and assessment instruments are provided. Your training department deploys directly from the program package.
